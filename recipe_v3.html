Khasi ko Masu

Goat curry, also known as "khasi ko masu" in Nepali, is a traditional dish that is mainly served during festivals in Nepal. The dish itself is interwined with the agriculture of the country. In Nepal, rearing goats is a common thing villagers have done for years. Over time families started to come up with recipes including goat. The use of local spices and herbs is an important aspect of the dish's history, with each region having its variations. The process of cooking the dish itself is a cultural tradition in many Nepali families, often passed down through generations.

Ingredients

  • Goat meat, bone in - 2lbs
  • Large onions, finely chopped - 3
  • Ginger & garlic paste - 2tbs
  • Tumeric powder- 1tsp
  • Red chili powder - 1tsp
  • Coriander powder - 2tsp
  • Cumin powder- 1tsp
  • Salt - 2tsp
  • Garam masala - 1 1/2tsp
  • Bay leaves - 2
  • Cardamom pods - 4
  • Water - 2cups
  • Ghee - 4tsp

Directions

  1. Heat ghee in a pressure cooker.
  2. Add bay leaves, onions, and cardamom pods. Sauté until onions are golden brown.
  3. Add ginger and garlic paste. Sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add turmeric powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, red chili powder, and salt. Sauté for a couple of minutes until the spices are fragrant and well combined.
  5. Add the goat pieces and stir well to coat them with the spice mixture.
  6. Cook the mutton over medium heat for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, allowing it to sear and develop flavor.
  7. Add enough water to cover the meat. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low.
  8. Cover and simmer until the mutton is very tender.
  9. Once the mutton is tender, add garam masala and simmer for 5 more minutes.
  10. Serve with rice or naan and eat.
